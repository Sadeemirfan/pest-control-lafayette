Common Pest Challenges in Scott, LA

Understanding the specific threats Scott residents face helps us deliver more effective solutions.

Subterranean Termites

Scott sits in one of the highest termite-pressure zones in the entire United States. Formosan and native subterranean termites are active throughout the year, with major swarm events occurring in spring and early summer. The warm, moist soil conditions around Scott are ideal for termite colony expansion, and the large number of older wood-frame homes in established neighborhoods means there is no shortage of food sources. Left untreated, termite colonies can cause tens of thousands of dollars in structural damage within just a few years. Annual inspections and proactive treatment are essential for every Scott property owner.

Cockroaches

Both German cockroaches and American cockroaches (commonly called “palmetto bugs” locally) are major problems in Scott. German cockroaches infest kitchens, bathrooms, and commercial food service areas, reproducing rapidly and spreading bacteria to food preparation surfaces. American cockroaches tend to live in sewers, crawl spaces, and utility areas but regularly invade homes through drains, gaps in foundations, and poorly sealed doors. Scott's warm, humid conditions allow cockroach populations to thrive all year, making professional treatment and ongoing prevention essential rather than optional.

Fire Ants and Carpenter Ants

Red imported fire ants are ubiquitous throughout Scott, colonizing lawns, landscaping, sidewalk cracks, and utility areas. Their mounds can appear seemingly overnight and pose a real danger to children, pets, and anyone who accidentally disturbs a colony. Carpenter ants present a different threat — they tunnel through moist or decaying wood in homes, potentially causing structural damage over time. Scott's frequent rainfall and high moisture levels mean that wood in crawl spaces, soffits, and around window frames is often susceptible to the moisture damage that attracts carpenter ants.

Mosquitoes

Mosquitoes are a fact of life in South Louisiana, but Scott's proximity to drainage canals, retention ponds, and low-lying areas makes mosquito pressure particularly intense here. The mosquito season runs from approximately March through November, with populations exploding after any significant rainfall. In addition to being a nuisance, mosquitoes in our region carry real health risks including West Nile virus and other vector-borne illnesses. Professional mosquito control programs that target both adult mosquitoes and larval breeding sites can dramatically reduce populations and make your yard usable again.

Rodents

Norway rats, roof rats, and house mice are common throughout Scott. Roof rats are especially prevalent in older residential areas with mature tree canopy, as they use tree branches and utility lines to access rooftops and attic spaces. Norway rats are more likely to burrow around foundations and enter homes through gaps at ground level. Rodents contaminate food, chew electrical wiring (a significant fire hazard), damage insulation, and spread disease through their droppings and urine. Scott's combination of residential neighborhoods, commercial food establishments, and abundant landscaping provides ideal habitat for rodent populations.

Bed Bugs

Bed bug activity has increased significantly throughout Louisiana over the past decade, and Scott is no exception. These parasitic insects hitchhike into homes through luggage, used furniture, clothing, and visitors. They are not a sign of poor housekeeping — bed bugs affect clean homes and upscale hotels alike. Once established, bed bug populations are extremely difficult to eliminate without professional treatment. Our heat treatment and chemical treatment protocols are designed to eliminate all life stages of bed bugs, including eggs, in a single comprehensive service or a planned series of treatments depending on the severity of infestation.
