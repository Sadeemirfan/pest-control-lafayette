Know Your Enemy

Bed Bug Identification: What You Need to Know

Bed bugs (Cimex lectularius) are small, flat, reddish-brown insects about the size of an apple seed—roughly 5 to 7 millimeters long. Despite their name, bed bugs don't live only in beds. They hide in the seams of mattresses, box springs, headboards, bed frames, nightstands, couches, luggage, electrical outlets, behind baseboards, and in the folds of curtains. They are expert hiders, capable of squeezing into spaces as thin as a credit card.

In Lafayette, LA, our warm, humid subtropical climate creates near-ideal conditions for bed bug activity year-round. Unlike some pests that slow down in cooler months, bed bugs thrive at room temperature and remain active throughout the year in our region. Their ability to survive for months without feeding makes them extraordinarily difficult to eliminate without professional intervention.

Bed bugs are nocturnal feeders. They emerge at night—typically in the hours before dawn—to feed on sleeping humans, attracted by body heat and the carbon dioxide we exhale. A single female bed bug can lay 200 to 500 eggs during her lifetime, meaning a small infestation can become a major problem in a matter of weeks.

Common Signs of a Bed Bug Infestation

  • Small, itchy red bites in a line or cluster on skin exposed during sleep—often on arms, neck, face, and legs
  • Tiny dark brown or black fecal spots on mattress seams, sheets, and nearby walls
  • Rust-colored blood stains on pillowcases or sheets from crushed bugs or feeding activity
  • Shed exoskeletons (molted skins) in mattress seams, furniture joints, and baseboards
  • Live bugs—small, oval, reddish-brown insects found in mattress seams, box spring folds, and furniture crevices
  • A distinctive musty, sweet odor—often described as overripe berries—in heavily infested rooms
  • Eggs and eggshells: tiny (1mm), pale yellow casings tucked into cracks and seams
  • Waking up with new, unexplained bites that weren't present when you went to sleep
  • Finding bugs in luggage after returning from travel, hotel stays, or visiting infested locations

Spread & Transmission

How Bed Bugs Enter Lafayette Homes

Bed bugs are expert hitchhikers. They don't fly or jump, but they travel remarkably well by clinging to clothing, luggage, furniture, and personal belongings. Understanding how they spread is the first step to prevention.

âï¸

Travel & Hotels

Lafayette sits along the I-10 corridor—one of the busiest highways in the South. Business travelers, tourists visiting Acadiana's restaurants and music scene, and guests at our numerous hotels can unknowingly carry bed bugs home in their luggage. Always inspect hotel room mattresses and headboards, and keep luggage off the floor when traveling.

ð

University Housing

UL Lafayette's student population experiences significant turnover every semester. Dormitories, off-campus apartments, and shared housing near campus are common hotspots for bed bug activity. Students moving used furniture, sharing spaces, or sleeping in infested dorms can bring bed bugs into family homes during breaks.

ðï¸

Used Furniture & Thrift Stores

Buying second-hand furniture, mattresses, or upholstered items is one of the most common ways bed bugs enter a home. Bed bugs and their eggs can remain dormant in furniture fabric and wooden joints for months. Never accept or purchase used mattresses, and thoroughly inspect any second-hand upholstered furniture before bringing it inside.

Guests & Visitors

Bed bugs can hitch rides in the clothing or bags of guests who have an active infestation in their own home. This is especially common in multi-family buildings and apartment complexes throughout Lafayette, Carencro, and the greater Acadiana area, where they can migrate through wall voids and shared plumbing spaces between units.

ð¨

Healthcare & Care Facilities

Long-term care facilities, hospitals, and rehabilitation centers can harbor bed bugs that travel home with patients and visitors. Lafayette's growing healthcare sector means this is an increasingly relevant concern. Healthcare workers and frequent visitors to such facilities should inspect themselves and their belongings regularly.

ð¦

Laundromats & Shared Spaces

Public laundromats, shared laundry rooms in apartment complexes, and even movie theater seats can harbor bed bugs. They can transfer from infested clothing in shared laundry carts or folding tables. Transporting laundry in sealed bags and not folding clothes on public surfaces reduces this risk significantly.

Health & Well-Being

Health Risks of a Bed Bug Infestation

While bed bugs are not known to transmit infectious diseases directly, the health consequences of a bed bug infestation are real, serious, and wide-ranging. The physical and psychological toll of living with an active infestation should not be underestimated, and prompt professional treatment is always the right choice.

Bite reactions vary significantly between individuals. Some people show no visible skin reaction at all, making infestations harder to detect early. Others develop intense allergic reactions requiring medical attention. The bites typically appear as small, raised, red welts that can be intensely itchy. Scratching can break the skin, leading to secondary bacterial infections—a real concern in Louisiana's warm, humid climate.

The psychological impact of bed bug infestations is well-documented in medical literature. Victims often develop anxiety, hypervigilance, and insomnia—the inability to sleep knowing something is biting them at night. Chronic sleep deprivation leads to impaired cognitive function, weakened immunity, mood disorders, and reduced quality of life. Some individuals develop PTSD-like symptoms even after the infestation has been eliminated.

How to Prepare for Bed Bug Treatment

Proper preparation before your bed bug treatment is critical to the success of the service. Inadequate preparation is one of the most common reasons bed bug treatments fail or require additional visits. Our technicians will provide you with a detailed preparation checklist specific to your treatment type, but here are the general guidelines for most chemical treatment protocols.

For heat treatment, preparation requirements are considerably simpler. The primary tasks involve removing or protecting heat-sensitive items that could be damaged by high temperatures. Your Acadiana Pest Solutions technician will provide a specific list for your situation.

ð¡ Pro Tip for Lafayette Residents

Do NOT throw out your furniture before treatment. Discarding infested mattresses or furniture and replacing them before treatment is completed can actually spread bed bugs to other parts of your home or to your neighbors. Let us treat first, then decide on furniture replacement with our guidance.

ð« What NOT to Do

Never apply over-the-counter sprays or bug bombs before a professional treatment. These often drive bed bugs deeper into wall voids and further into furniture, making them harder to reach with professional treatments. They can also create chemical resistance issues. Call a professional first.

Chemical Treatment Preparation Checklist

  • Wash all bedding, linens, pillowcases, and clothing in hot water (130Â°F+) and dry on high heat for at least 30 minutes
  • Place all washed items in sealed plastic bags immediately after drying and store in an untreated area
  • Remove all items from under beds, from closet floors, and from furniture drawers
  • Vacuum all carpeted areas, mattresses, and upholstered furniture thoroughly, then seal and dispose of the vacuum bag outside immediately
  • Move furniture away from walls by at least 12 inches to allow technician access
  • Remove all pets, fish tanks (or cover and turn off), and people from the home for the duration of treatment and the re-entry period specified by your technician (typically 2 to 4 hours)
  • Inform your technician of any pre-existing health conditions, particularly respiratory issues or chemical sensitivities
  • Do NOT move furniture or belongings from infested rooms to uninfested rooms, as this can spread bed bugs
  • Leave dresser drawers slightly open so treatment can penetrate inside
  • Declutter as much as possible—reduce hiding places to maximize treatment contact
Stay Protected

Bed Bug Prevention Tips for Lafayette Residents

The best bed bug treatment is the one you never need. These practical prevention strategies are especially important for Lafayette and Acadiana residents given our region's unique risk factors.

ð§³

Smart Travel Habits

When staying at hotels along Ambassador Caffery, the I-10 corridor, or anywhere else—inspect the mattress seams, headboard, and nightstands before unpacking. Keep luggage on luggage racks, not the floor or bed. When you return home, unpack your suitcase outside or in the garage, wash all clothing immediately on hot, and inspect your bags before storing them.

ð¡ï¸

Mattress Encasements

Install quality mattress and box spring encasements specifically labeled for bed bugs. These zippered covers trap any existing bed bugs inside (where they eventually die) and make future detection much easier—bed bugs are visible on a white encasement surface. Once installed, leave them on permanently. They also protect against moisture and allergens in our humid Louisiana climate.

ð

Inspect Secondhand Items

Before bringing any used furniture, mattress, or upholstered item into your home, conduct a thorough inspection of all seams, joints, and crevices. Use a flashlight and a credit card to check all folds. Better yet, avoid secondhand upholstered furniture entirely if you're in an area with known bed bug activity. Wooden furniture can be treated with a steamer before bringing inside.

ð¢

Report & Communicate

If you live in an apartment building in Lafayette or Carencro, report any signs of bed bugs to your property manager immediately. In multi-unit buildings, bed bugs spread quickly between units through shared walls, electrical conduit, and plumbing chases. Early reporting enables building-wide treatment that protects everyone. Tenants have legal rights to pest-free habitation in Louisiana.

ð 

Reduce Clutter

Clutter provides additional hiding places for bed bugs and makes inspections and treatments significantly less effective. Keep bedrooms organized, reduce piles of clothing and bedding on floors, and store off-season items in sealed plastic bins rather than cardboard boxes. Regular vacuuming of mattress surfaces and carpet edges can catch early-stage infestations.

ð

Schedule Regular Inspections

For high-risk properties—rental units, university-area apartments, Airbnb rentals, and homes with frequent guests—schedule periodic professional bed bug inspections. Early detection is dramatically cheaper and easier to treat than a widespread infestation. Acadiana Pest Solutions offers preventive inspection programs for property managers throughout the Lafayette area.

How quickly can you treat my Lafayette home after I call? +
In most cases, we can schedule a same-day or next-day inspection for Lafayette and surrounding Acadiana communities. Following inspection and treatment planning, heat treatments can often be scheduled within 1 to 3 business days. Chemical treatments can sometimes begin at the initial inspection visit if the home is already prepared. We understand the urgency of bed bug situations and prioritize getting to you as quickly as possible. Call (337) 555-0199 and we'll find the earliest available slot.
Is heat treatment safe for my family, pets, and belongings? +
Heat treatment is among the safest bed bug treatment methods available when performed by trained professionals. There are no chemical residues and no off-gassing concerns, making it ideal for households with young children, pregnant women, people with respiratory sensitivities, and pets. However, certain items are heat-sensitive and must be removed before treatment, including candles, aerosol cans, vinyl records, some medications, certain electronics, plants, and pets (obviously). Your technician will provide a comprehensive list of items to protect or remove before treatment day. You and your family (including pets) must vacate during treatment but can typically return 30 to 60 minutes after equipment is removed and the home has cooled to a comfortable temperature.
How do I know if bed bugs came from a hotel or from used furniture I bought? +
Pinpointing the exact source of a bed bug infestation is often difficult but helps prevent re-infestation after treatment. The timeline can provide clues: if bites and signs appeared within 2 to 3 weeks of a hotel stay, travel is likely the culprit. If the infestation seems concentrated around a specific piece of recently acquired furniture, that's your most probable source. Our inspection process helps narrow down likely introduction points, and we'll discuss your recent activities—travel along the I-10 corridor, visits to University of Lafayette area, recent furniture purchases—to help identify and address the source. Regardless of the source, the treatment approach is the same, but identifying the source helps prevent recurrence.
Can I save my mattress, or do I need to throw everything out? +
In most cases, you do NOT need to discard your mattress or other furniture before or after professional treatment. This is one of the most common misconceptions about bed bug treatment. Both heat treatment and properly applied chemical treatment can eliminate bed bugs living in and on mattresses. After treatment, encasing your mattress and box spring in quality bed bug-proof encasements protects your investment and makes future monitoring easier. We strongly advise against discarding furniture before consulting with us—moving infested furniture out of the home can spread bed bugs to other parts of the house or to neighbors and may actually complicate treatment by scattering the population.
As a Lafayette landlord, am I responsible for bed bug treatment in my rental properties? +
Louisiana law requires landlords to provide habitable rental properties, which includes properties free from pest infestations that affect health and safety. Bed bugs generally fall into this category. However, the specific responsibilities can depend on the circumstances—whether the infestation was present at move-in, how the infestation was introduced, and the language of the lease agreement. As a general rule, if bed bugs were present before a tenant moved in or spread from another unit, the landlord is typically responsible. If a tenant introduced the infestation, tenant responsibility may apply. We work regularly with Lafayette area property managers and landlords to provide discreet, effective treatment for multi-unit properties and to document treatment for compliance purposes. Call us to discuss your specific property situation.
