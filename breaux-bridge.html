Know Your Enemy

Common Pests in Breaux Bridge, LA

Breaux Bridge’s position between the Atchafalaya Basin and the rest of the Acadiana prairie makes it a hotspot for a wide variety of pests. Here are the six most common threats our technicians encounter in this area:

🐜

Subterranean Termites

Breaux Bridge’s historic downtown buildings — many dating to the 19th century — are especially vulnerable to subterranean termite damage. The moist, clay-heavy soils along Bayou Teche provide perfect tunneling conditions for Formosan and native termite colonies. Older wooden structures with minimal vapor barriers are at the greatest risk. Left untreated, a mature Formosan termite colony can consume a pound of wood per day, causing structural damage that can run into tens of thousands of dollars. If you own property in or near the historic district, a professional termite inspection is strongly recommended.

🦟

Mosquitoes

Living near the Atchafalaya Basin means living with mosquitoes — that’s just a fact of life in Breaux Bridge. The basin’s extensive network of backwater channels, sloughs, and cypress swamps provides virtually unlimited breeding habitat for mosquitoes of multiple species, some of which can carry West Nile virus and other pathogens. Standing water near homes, drainage ditches along rural roads, and even ornamental water features become rapid mosquito nurseries during the long, hot Louisiana summers. Our mosquito control treatments target breeding sites and resting areas to give your yard back to you.

🐀

Rodents

Rats and mice are a persistent problem in Breaux Bridge, particularly in and around older commercial and residential buildings near the town center. Norway rats and roof rats are both active in the area, using the bayou corridors as travel routes and foraging for food in dumpsters, gardens, and poorly sealed pantries. As fall temperatures drop, rodents push into homes seeking warmth and shelter, chewing through insulation, electrical wiring, and plumbing. Properties near agricultural fields on the outskirts of town can see pressure from field mice as well, especially after harvest season.

🪳

Cockroaches

The American cockroach — known locally as the “palmetto bug” — thrives in Breaux Bridge’s humid climate. These large, reddish-brown insects prefer moist environments like sewers, crawl spaces under pier-and-beam homes, and the humid areas behind appliances. German cockroaches, though smaller, are even more problematic when they infest kitchens and restaurants. Given Breaux Bridge’s active food and hospitality scene along Main Street, cockroach pressure in commercial spaces is a real concern. Our targeted cockroach control programs eliminate infestations and prevent re-entry.

🐜

Fire Ants & Carpenter Ants

Fire ants are ubiquitous throughout St. Martin Parish, and Breaux Bridge is no exception. Their large mounds appear in lawns, pastures, and gardens, posing a painful — and sometimes dangerous — threat to children, pets, and livestock. In addition, carpenter ants have become increasingly common in older Breaux Bridge homes where moisture-damaged wood provides ideal nesting material. Unlike termites, carpenter ants don’t eat wood, but they hollow it out for their galleries, causing significant structural weakening over time in sills, joists, and framing exposed to repeated moisture.

🕷

Spiders & Other Moisture Pests

Brown recluse and black widow spiders are both found in the Breaux Bridge area, hiding in undisturbed spaces like attics, garages, woodpiles, and the crawl spaces beneath older homes. Beyond spiders, Breaux Bridge’s moisture-rich environment attracts a range of nuisance pests including silverfish, centipedes, and earwigs that thrive in damp conditions. Homes with poor drainage, crawl spaces without moisture barriers, or aging foundation seals are particularly susceptible. Addressing moisture issues alongside pest control is key to long-term success in this climate.
