Cockroach Infestations in Acadiana: Why They’re So Persistent

Lafayette, Louisiana sits in one of the most cockroach-friendly climates on the continent. Warm temperatures that rarely dip below freezing, high ambient humidity, abundant food sources, and older construction with countless entry points all combine to create ideal conditions for roach populations to explode. Unlike northern states where a hard winter kills off many insect pests, Acadiana’s mild winters allow cockroaches to breed year-round, making it nearly impossible to solve a roach problem with a single treatment.

Cockroach Species Common to the Acadiana Region

Not all cockroaches are the same. Different species have different habits, different harborage preferences, and respond differently to treatment products. Correct identification is the first step toward effective control. Our inspectors are trained to identify the species present and tailor a treatment plan accordingly.

Most Common

German Cockroach (Blattella germanica)

The number-one cockroach pest in homes and restaurants across Louisiana. Small (half to five-eighths of an inch), tan to light brown, with two parallel dark stripes behind the head. German roaches reproduce extremely rapidly — a single female can produce over 30,000 descendants per year. They prefer warm, humid spaces near food and water: kitchen cabinets, under appliances, behind stoves and refrigerators, and inside walls near plumbing. German roaches are notoriously difficult to eliminate without professional treatment.

Large Invader

American Cockroach (Periplaneta americana)

Often called the “palmetto bug” in Louisiana, this is the largest common roach in the region, reaching 1.5 to 2 inches in length. Reddish-brown with a yellowish figure-eight pattern on the back of the head. American roaches typically live outdoors in humid environments — mulch beds, tree holes, leaf litter, utility tunnels — and enter homes through gaps under doors, around pipes, and through sewer systems. They are particularly active during warm months and at night.

Wood & Yard

Smokybrown Cockroach (Periplaneta fuliginosa)

Common in the humid Gulf Coast region, the smokybrown roach is shiny dark mahogany to black in color and slightly smaller than the American cockroach. It is strongly attracted to light and is often found flying toward exterior lights at night. Outdoors, it harborages in wood piles, attic vents, tree holes, and dense landscaping. It enters structures through gaps and vents and is commonly found in attics, garages, and wall voids.

Outdoor Species

Brown-Banded Cockroach (Supella longipalpa)

Smaller than the German roach and often confused with it, the brown-banded cockroach has distinctive light tan bands across its wings. Unlike German roaches, it does not require the same level of moisture and can be found throughout the home — inside electronics, in closets, under furniture, behind picture frames, and in bedrooms. This species can be particularly challenging to control because it spreads widely and lays eggs in hard-to-reach locations.

Sewer Dweller

Oriental Cockroach (Blatta orientalis)

Dark brown to nearly black and roughly one inch long, the Oriental cockroach prefers very damp conditions and is commonly associated with drains, sewers, crawl spaces, and basements. It is sometimes called the “water bug.” While less common than German and American roaches in Acadiana, it is found in commercial properties, older homes with crawl spaces, and buildings near bayous and drainage ditches. It produces a strong, musty odor and is considered one of the dirtiest of all cockroach species.

Outdoor Flyer

Florida Woods Cockroach (Eurycotis floridana)

Known locally as the “stinking roach” or “Florida skunk roach,” this species is found in the more southeastern parts of Acadiana near wooded and swampy areas. It is large, dark, and almost wingless. While it does not typically infest indoors, it can enter homes by accident and is notable for emitting a foul-smelling defensive secretion when disturbed. Landscaping management and perimeter treatment are the keys to keeping this species outside.

Health Risks of Cockroach Infestations

Cockroaches are more than a nuisance — they pose serious, documented health risks to your family. They carry and transmit dozens of pathogenic organisms including:

  • Salmonella typhi — the bacterium responsible for typhoid fever, transmitted by roaches contaminating food preparation surfaces.
  • Escherichia coli (E. coli) — spread via roach feces and body contact with food and utensils.
  • Staphylococcus and other bacteria that cause food poisoning and gastrointestinal illness.
  • Allergens and asthma triggers — cockroach saliva, feces, shed skins, and egg cases are potent allergens. Studies consistently link cockroach infestations to elevated rates of asthma, particularly in children. The Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America identifies cockroach allergens as one of the most significant indoor asthma triggers.
  • Poliomyelitis virus — cockroaches have been documented as mechanical vectors in environments with poor sanitation.
  • Dysentery, diarrhea, and cholera organisms have all been isolated from cockroach bodies in field studies.

Beyond pathogen transmission, cockroaches contaminate food with their feces and shed skins, damage paper goods and fabrics, and cause psychological distress. In restaurant and food-service settings, a cockroach sighting can result in health department citations, temporary closure, and lasting reputational damage. Signs You Have a Cockroach Infestation

Cockroaches are nocturnal and expert at hiding. By the time you see live roaches during daylight hours, the infestation is usually well established. Learn to recognize the early warning signs so you can call for treatment before the population explodes.

Live roaches seen during the day
Dark, pepper-like droppings on shelves or under appliances
Musty or oily odor in the kitchen or bathroom
Egg cases (oothecae) behind appliances or in cracks
Shed skins (exuviae) in dark corners or cabinets
Smear marks on walls along roach pathways
Chewed or damaged food packaging
Scratch marks on soap or wax candles
Dead roaches in hidden areas
Neighbors reporting roach problems

German cockroach infestations, in particular, can grow from a handful of insects to thousands in just a few months because their reproductive rate is so high. Our Cockroach Treatment Process

We do not simply spray and hope for the best. Our cockroach control program uses an Integrated Pest Management (IPM) approach that combines thorough inspection, targeted treatment products, and follow-up monitoring. Every treatment plan is customized to the species involved, the severity of the infestation, and the specific layout of your home or business.

1

Comprehensive Inspection

Before we apply a single product, we perform a detailed inspection of your entire property. We check behind and under all appliances, inside cabinets and pantries, along plumbing penetrations, inside electrical outlets and junction boxes, under sinks, in crawl spaces, and along the exterior perimeter. We identify the species present, locate primary harborage areas, assess entry points, and evaluate conducive conditions (food and moisture sources) that are sustaining the population. Our inspection report becomes the roadmap for your treatment plan.

2

Precision Gel Bait Applications

For German cockroaches and brown-banded roaches, professional-grade gel baits are among the most effective tools available. We apply small, targeted placements of slow-acting bait gel directly in roach harborage areas — inside cabinet hinges, along crack-and-crevice areas, behind appliances, and near plumbing. Cockroaches consume the bait, return to the harborage, and die. Nestmates consume the dead and dying roaches and are also killed through secondary poisoning. Unlike sprays, gels do not cause roaches to scatter and can eliminate entire colonies. We use professional-grade products not available in retail stores, rotating active ingredients regularly to prevent resistance development.

3

Residual Insecticide Applications

For American roaches, smokybrown roaches, and perimeter control, we apply professional residual insecticides to harborage zones and entry points. These products leave a lasting residual barrier that kills roaches on contact for weeks after application. We treat exterior foundation walls, entry points around pipes and conduits, garage doors, expansion joints, mulch beds, and other outdoor harborage areas. Interior residual treatments are applied in attic spaces, wall voids, crawl spaces, and utility areas as appropriate. All products used by Acadiana Pest Solutions are EPA-registered, applied according to label directions by licensed professionals.

4

Insect Growth Regulator (IGR) Treatment

Insect Growth Regulators are one of the most powerful tools in the professional pest control arsenal, yet they remain largely unknown to consumers. IGRs mimic juvenile insect hormones, disrupting the normal development of cockroach nymphs and preventing them from reaching reproductive maturity. Even if an adult roach survives the initial treatment, IGR exposure ensures its offspring cannot reproduce. We apply IGR products as a spray, aerosol, or incorporated into bait formulations depending on the situation. IGRs are extremely low in mammalian toxicity, making them an excellent fit for homes with children and pets. Used in combination with bait and residuals, IGRs dramatically accelerate the collapse of cockroach populations and help prevent re-infestation.

5

Dust Treatments for Voids and Cracks

For cockroaches living deep inside wall voids, electrical panels, under-floor spaces, and attics, dust formulations (boric acid and professional insecticidal dusts) provide long-lasting control in areas that liquid products cannot reach effectively. Our technicians inject dust into void spaces using specialized equipment to ensure even distribution. These dust treatments remain effective for months in dry, protected voids, providing ongoing control even after other products have broken down.

6

Exclusion and Sanitation Recommendations

Treatment without addressing root causes leads to re-infestation. After each service visit, your technician provides a written report identifying conditions that are contributing to the problem and recommending corrective actions. Common recommendations include sealing gaps around plumbing penetrations, fixing dripping faucets or condensation issues, eliminating food debris from hard-to-clean areas, properly sealing food storage containers, and reducing clutter that provides harborage. For major entry points such as gaps under doors, around utility conduits, or in the foundation, we can provide exclusion services or refer you to trusted contractors.

7

Follow-Up Monitoring and Re-Treatment

We do not consider our job done until your cockroach problem is fully resolved. Heavy German roach infestations in particular may require two or three treatment visits over four to six weeks to achieve complete control. Between visits, we use monitoring glue boards strategically placed in harborage areas to track roach activity, confirm the treatment is working, and identify any remaining hotspots that need additional attention. Our technicians review the traps at each visit and adjust the treatment strategy accordingly. We stand behind our work — if you see roaches between scheduled service visits, call us and we will return at no additional charge.

Why Over-the-Counter Products Fail

Walk down the pest control aisle at any home improvement store and you will find dozens of cockroach sprays, traps, bombs, and bait products. Many homeowners spend significant time and money on these products before finally calling a professional — often after the infestation has grown substantially larger than it was when they first noticed it. There are several reasons why DIY approaches frequently fail for cockroach control.

  • Repellent sprays scatter roaches rather than killing them at the source. When you spray a cabinet with a repellent product, the roaches simply move to another part of the structure. The population is not reduced — it is just redistributed.
  • Consumer-grade baits use lower-concentration active ingredients than professional products and may not be placed in the optimal locations. Proper bait placement requires knowledge of roach behavior and harborage patterns.
  • Bug bombs and foggers rarely reach harborage areas where roaches actually live. Cockroaches retreat deep into cracks and voids when they detect the fog, surviving the treatment. Foggers also leave a residue on exposed surfaces that can be a health concern and may actually repel future bait placements.
  • No IGR component means that surviving roaches and egg cases continue to produce new generations, quickly replenishing any population reduction achieved by consumer products.
  • Resistance is a real problem. Some German cockroach populations in Louisiana have developed resistance to pyrethrins and other active ingredients commonly found in retail products. Professional products include a broader range of active ingredients, and professional applicators rotate chemistries to manage resistance.

The bottom line: for anything beyond a minor, isolated occurrence, professional cockroach treatment is almost always faster, more cost-effective over time, and longer-lasting than repeated DIY attempts. Call Acadiana Pest Solutions and get it done right the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to eliminate a cockroach infestation? +
For a moderate German roach infestation, most customers see 70 to 90 percent reduction within the first week after treatment, with complete elimination typically achieved within two to four weeks. Severe infestations may require two or three treatment visits over four to six weeks. American and smokybrown roaches respond more quickly because they are not reproducing inside the structure at the same rate. Your technician will give you a realistic timeline after the initial inspection.
Is cockroach treatment safe for my children and pets? +
Yes, when applied by a licensed professional according to label directions. We use EPA-registered products with well-established safety profiles. After treatment, we advise keeping children and pets out of treated areas until they are completely dry (typically 30 to 60 minutes for liquid applications). Gel bait products are applied in small, targeted placements inside cracks and behind appliances where children and pets cannot easily access them. We are always happy to discuss the specific products we are using and answer any questions you have about safety before, during, or after treatment.
Do I need to leave my home during treatment? +
For a standard gel bait and residual treatment, it is not necessary to leave your home, though we do ask that you keep children and pets out of rooms being treated until the product dries. If we are performing a more intensive treatment such as a void dust application or treating a heavily infested kitchen, we may ask you to stay out of certain areas for a short period. We will discuss preparation requirements and any temporary restrictions with you when you schedule your appointment.
Why am I seeing MORE roaches after treatment? +
This is completely normal and is actually a sign that the treatment is working. Gel baits cause cockroaches to come out of hiding to feed on the bait, and insecticides cause them to flee harborage areas. You may see increased activity for the first three to seven days after treatment. After that, activity should decline rapidly. If you are still seeing significant activity after ten to fourteen days, contact us and we will schedule a follow-up visit at no additional charge.
Can I clean my kitchen after cockroach treatment? +
We recommend waiting at least 48 to 72 hours before mopping floors or wiping down surfaces in treated areas to allow the residual products to cure properly. Do not wipe down the areas where gel bait has been placed — doing so will remove the bait and reduce effectiveness. Light surface cleaning of food preparation areas is fine. Your technician will give you specific aftercare instructions based on the products used in your home.
Are German roaches and palmetto bugs the same? +
“Palmetto bug” is a common colloquial name used throughout the Gulf South, but it typically refers to the American cockroach — the large, reddish-brown roach that comes in from outside and is found in bathrooms, kitchens, and garages. German cockroaches are much smaller (about half an inch), light tan, and are almost always found indoors living in tight harborage areas near food and moisture. They are separate species that require different treatment approaches. Both are very common in Acadiana, and many homes have both species present simultaneously.
What preparation do I need to do before cockroach treatment? +
For best results, we ask that you pull appliances slightly away from the wall if possible, remove items from under sinks, empty the lower shelves of kitchen cabinets, and secure or remove pet food. Avoid spraying any over-the-counter pesticides for at least five to seven days before your appointment, as repellent products can interfere with bait acceptance. The more accessible the harborage areas are, the more thoroughly we can treat. Our office staff will provide a complete preparation checklist when you schedule your appointment.
