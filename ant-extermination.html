Imported Red Fire Ant (Solenopsis invicta)

The red imported fire ant is by far the most significant ant pest in Louisiana. These reddish-brown ants build distinctive dome-shaped mounds in open, sunny areas Ã¢ÂÂ lawns, playgrounds, parks, and along sidewalks. Fire ant mounds can reach 18 inches high and contain colonies of 100,000 to 500,000 workers. Odorous house ants are small (1/16 to Ã¢ÂÂ inch), dark brown to black ants named for the rotten coconut smell they emit when crushed. Prevention

Ant Prevention Tips for Lafayette Homeowners

Even after professional treatment, proactive prevention is essential to keep ants from returning to your Acadiana home. Here are the most effective strategies our experts recommend for Lafayette's unique climate and landscape conditions.

Outdoor Prevention

  • Keep mulch 12Ã¢ÂÂ18 inches from the foundation Deep landscape mulch retains moisture and warmth Ã¢ÂÂ perfect conditions for ant nesting. Pull mulch back from your foundation and exterior walls.
  • Eliminate standing water and drainage issues Fire ants and carpenter ants are attracted to moisture. Correct drainage grading, fix leaking irrigation, and empty any standing water around the yard after rain.
  • Remove wood-to-soil contact Firewood stacks, lumber piles, tree stumps, and wooden landscape timbers in direct contact with soil are prime carpenter ant nesting sites. Store firewood at least 20 feet from your home on a raised rack.
  • Trim vegetation away from the home Tree branches, shrubs, and vines touching your roofline or siding create ant highways into your home. Keep vegetation at least 18 inches from all exterior walls.
  • Monitor your lawn regularly Walk your yard after rain events when fire ant mounds become most visible. Early detection of new mounds allows for faster treatment before colonies grow large.
  • Treat new mounds immediately Don't step on or disturb a fire ant mound Ã¢ÂÂ call us or apply a labeled mound treatment right away. Disturbing a mound without treatment just disperses the colony, making it harder to eliminate.

Indoor Prevention

  • Seal all entry points Caulk gaps around window frames, door frames, pipe penetrations, and utility lines entering the home. Even tiny cracks Ã¢ÂÂ as small as 1/32 inch Ã¢ÂÂ are large enough for odorous house ants to enter.
  • Store food in airtight containers Ants are attracted to sugars, proteins, and greasy foods. Keep all pantry items in sealed containers and never leave pet food out overnight.
  • Clean up food residue promptly Wipe down counters after cooking, sweep up crumbs, and don't let dirty dishes sit in the sink. Even the residue inside a recycling bin can attract foraging ants.
  • Fix moisture problems inside Leaking pipes under sinks, high bathroom humidity, and wet wood around HVAC condensation drains attract carpenter ants indoors. Repair leaks promptly and use a dehumidifier in damp areas.
  • Don't use repellent sprays over ant trails Over-the-counter ant sprays kill visible workers but alert the colony, causing it to split into multiple satellite colonies. Call a professional for gel baiting strategies that work with the ants' natural behavior.
  • Schedule annual preventive treatments In Louisiana's climate, annual perimeter treatments are the single most cost-effective way to prevent ant infestations from establishing inside your home.
