Common Pest Challenges in Broussard, LA

Understanding what you are up against is the first step to effective pest control.

[Termite]

Subterranean Termites

Broussard's warm, humid environment and clay-rich soils are ideal breeding grounds for Formosan and Eastern subterranean termites. These destructive insects can silently consume the structural wood of your home for years before visible damage becomes apparent. New construction homes with wood forms left in soil are especially vulnerable. We offer liquid barrier treatments and baiting systems to protect your investment.

[Mosquito]

Mosquitoes

Broussard's proximity to the Vermilion River, its numerous drainage ditches, retention ponds in newer subdivisions, and seasonal flooding make mosquito pressure intense from March through November. Mosquitoes are not just an annoyance — they are a genuine public health concern capable of transmitting West Nile virus, Eastern equine encephalitis, and other pathogens. Our barrier spray programs and larvicide treatments dramatically reduce mosquito populations on your property.

[Ant]

Fire Ants and Carpenter Ants

Red imported fire ants are ubiquitous throughout Broussard, colonizing lawns, parks, playgrounds, and the edges of driveways and sidewalks. Their painful stings are a serious concern for children and pets. Carpenter ants, attracted to moisture-damaged wood, are also common in older parts of Broussard. We treat both species with targeted baiting and contact insecticide programs that eliminate colonies at their source rather than just driving them to a new location on your property.

[Roach]

Cockroaches

American cockroaches, also known locally as “palmetto bugs,” are common throughout Broussard, particularly in older homes, restaurants, and commercial kitchens. German cockroaches are a persistent indoor pest that reproduce rapidly and are notoriously difficult to eliminate without professional treatment. Both species contaminate food, trigger allergies, and spread bacteria. Our cockroach control programs combine gel baiting, growth regulators, and exclusion work to eliminate infestations completely.

[Rodent]

Rats and Mice

As Broussard's new subdivisions push into previously rural land, displaced rodent populations move into attics, crawl spaces, and wall voids of area homes. Roof rats are especially problematic, entering homes through gaps around rooflines, utility penetrations, and HVAC systems. Rodents cause structural damage by gnawing on wiring and insulation, and they contaminate food and spread disease. We provide comprehensive rodent control including trapping, bait stations, and exclusion sealing to keep them out for good.

[Bug]

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs do not discriminate by neighborhood or home value — they are an equal-opportunity nuisance that hitchhikes on luggage, furniture, and clothing. Broussard's growing population of hospitality businesses and its connection to the broader Lafayette metro area means bed bug exposure is a real risk. Early detection and professional treatment are essential to stopping an infestation before it spreads. We offer thorough inspections and proven heat and chemical treatment protocols.
