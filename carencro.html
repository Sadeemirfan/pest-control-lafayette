Common Pests Found in Carencro, LA

Understanding local pest pressures is the first step to effective protection.

Subterranean Termites

Formosan and Eastern subterranean termites are a serious and ongoing threat to every structure in Carencro. Louisiana’s warm, moist soil is among the most termite-active environments in the entire country. A mature colony can consume several pounds of wood per day, causing damage that often goes undetected for years behind walls and under flooring. Annual inspections and a reliable termite baiting or liquid treatment program are essential for any Carencro property owner.

Mosquitoes

Mosquitoes are not just a nuisance — they are a genuine public health concern in Lafayette Parish. Standing water in ditches, low-lying yards, clogged gutters, and containers around your property all become breeding grounds for Aedes and Culex mosquito species that can carry diseases including West Nile Virus. Carencro’s drainage patterns and abundant rainfall from spring through fall keep mosquito populations sky-high without professional intervention.

Fire Ants

Red imported fire ants are deeply entrenched throughout Carencro’s residential lawns, schoolyards, and commercial properties. Their painful stings pose a real risk to children, pets, and anyone who accidentally disturbs a mound. Fire ant colonies can contain hundreds of thousands of workers and multiple queens, making over-the-counter treatments largely ineffective. Professional broadcast treatments and targeted mound baiting provide lasting control.

Cockroaches

American cockroaches, also commonly called palmetto bugs in south Louisiana, are large and prolific invaders of kitchens, bathrooms, and utility areas throughout Carencro. German cockroaches are a persistent indoor problem in apartments, restaurants, and homes. Both species reproduce rapidly and contaminate food surfaces with bacteria and allergens. Effective cockroach control requires treating harborage areas, applying gel baits, and sealing entry points to prevent reinfestation.

Rodents

Roof rats and Norway rats are active in Carencro, particularly as new construction displaces wildlife from surrounding areas. Rats and mice enter homes through surprisingly small gaps in rooflines, foundations, and utility penetrations. Once inside, they chew electrical wiring (creating fire hazards), contaminate insulation, and spread disease through droppings and urine. Our rodent programs include exclusion work that stops new animals from entering after existing populations are eliminated.

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs have become increasingly common across all of Acadiana, and Carencro is no exception. These blood-feeding insects hitchhike into homes via used furniture, luggage, and clothing. They hide in mattress seams, behind headboards, inside outlet covers, and in the cracks of bed frames. A bed bug infestation causes significant sleep disruption and anxiety. Our heat treatment and chemical treatment options eliminate bed bugs at all life stages quickly and effectively.

Do I need a termite bond in Carencro?

+

A termite bond, or termite warranty agreement, is highly recommended for any property in Louisiana, including Carencro. Given the extreme termite pressure in our region, a warranty agreement ensures that your property is inspected regularly and that any retreatment needed due to new termite activity is covered at no additional cost. Many lenders and home buyers require an active termite bond at the time of closing.

How many mosquito treatments will I need per season?

+

In Carencro, mosquito season effectively runs from March through October, with peak activity in the summer months. Most customers see the best results with monthly treatments during this period. We also offer bi-monthly service for properties with particularly high mosquito pressure. A single treatment typically provides meaningful relief for three to four weeks depending on rainfall and temperatures.

What should I do to prepare for a pest control visit?

+

For general pest control, we ask that you clear items away from baseboards in rooms being treated, store food items in sealed containers, and ensure pets are kept in an untreated area during and immediately after service. For termite treatments or rodent exclusion work, we may have additional specific preparation instructions that we will communicate when you schedule your appointment.
