The Hidden Health Dangers of a Cockroach Infestation

February 18, 2025 — 5 min read

Cockroaches are far more than just an unpleasant nuisance. These resilient pests are known carriers of at least 33 different kinds of bacteria, six species of parasitic worms, and seven other human pathogens. In homes with active cockroach populations, the allergens shed by cockroach saliva, feces, and decomposing bodies can trigger asthma attacks — especially in children. The American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine has documented a direct link between cockroach allergen exposure and increased emergency room visits for asthma patients. How Moisture Problems in Your Home Invite Pest Infestations

January 22, 2025 — 6 min read

Excess moisture is one of the single biggest factors that attracts pests to homes in Acadiana. South Louisiana's naturally high humidity already creates challenging conditions, but leaky pipes, inadequate ventilation in crawl spaces, and poor drainage around foundations can compound the problem significantly. Subterranean termites need moisture to survive and are drawn to damp wood. Cockroaches thrive in wet environments under sinks and behind appliances. Rodents seek out areas with easy access to water. Carpenter ants excavate galleries in water-damaged wood. This article covers the most common moisture issues found in Lafayette-area homes, how to identify them, and the repairs and improvements that can dramatically reduce your pest vulnerability while also preventing costly structural damage.

Flying Ants vs. Termites: How to Tell the Difference

December 5, 2024 — 4 min read

Every spring and fall, Acadiana homeowners spot swarms of winged insects around their homes and immediately wonder: are these flying ants, or termite swarmers? The answer matters enormously, because termite swarmers signal an active colony that may already be causing structural damage. Fortunately, there are several reliable physical differences between the two insects that you can observe with the naked eye. Termite swarmers have equal-length wings, a broad waist, and straight antennae. Flying ants have unequal wings, a pinched waist, and bent antennae. This identification guide uses clear, plain-language descriptions and a side-by-side comparison chart to help you quickly determine what you are looking at — and what steps to take next if termites are suspected.
